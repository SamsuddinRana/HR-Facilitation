Recognition and Praise Theory - Real World Scenario Based MCQs

1. Sarah consistently meets her project deadlines and helps her teammates. Her manager publicly praises her during a team meeting. What is the likely impact?

  • A. Sarah feels embarrassed
  • B. Sarah feels motivated and valued
  • C. Sarah ignores the praise
  • D. Sarah becomes overconfident
Answer: B
Public recognition boosts morale and reinforces positive behavior, making Sarah feel valued and motivated.

2. John receives a personalized thank-you email from his supervisor for his exceptional work on a client presentation. What aspect of recognition is being used?

  • A. Public praise
  • B. Monetary reward
  • C. Personalized praise
  • D. Team celebration
Answer: C
Personalized praise acknowledges individual contributions and strengthens employee engagement.

3. A manager praises the entire team for their collaborative effort in achieving a milestone. What is the benefit of this approach?

  • A. It creates competition
  • B. It fosters team unity
  • C. It reduces individual accountability
  • D. It delays project timelines
Answer: B
Recognizing team efforts promotes unity and encourages collaborative behavior.

4. Lisa's manager only gives praise during annual reviews. What is a potential drawback of this approach?

  • A. Employees feel overwhelmed
  • B. Praise loses its impact
  • C. Employees forget their achievements
  • D. Praise becomes more meaningful
Answer: B
Infrequent recognition can diminish its motivational impact; regular praise is more effective.

5. During a team lunch, a manager highlights Tom's innovative solution to a problem. What type of recognition is this?

  • A. Private praise
  • B. Public recognition
  • C. Monetary reward
  • D. Constructive feedback
Answer: B
Public recognition in informal settings reinforces positive behavior and boosts morale.

6. A company uses a peer-nomination system to recognize outstanding contributions. What is the advantage of this method?

  • A. Reduces manager workload
  • B. Encourages favoritism
  • C. Promotes peer appreciation
  • D. Limits recognition to top performers
Answer: C
Peer recognition fosters a culture of appreciation and strengthens team relationships.

8. A manager regularly praises employees for small achievements. What is the likely outcome?

  • A. Employees become complacent
  • B. Praise loses value
  • C. Employees feel consistently motivated
  • D. Employees ignore the praise
Answer: C
Frequent recognition maintains motivation and reinforces continuous improvement.

9. A team member is praised in front of clients for their dedication. What is the impact of external recognition?

  • A. It embarrasses the employee
  • B. It enhances employee reputation
  • C. It creates pressure
  • D. It undermines team effort
Answer: B
External recognition boosts employee confidence and enhances their professional reputation.

10. A manager uses recognition to highlight behaviors aligned with company values. What is the strategic benefit?

  • A. It confuses employees
  • B. It reinforces desired behaviors
  • C. It delays performance reviews
  • D. It reduces employee autonomy
Answer: B
Strategic recognition aligns employee actions with organizational goals and values.
