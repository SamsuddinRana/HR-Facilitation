McGregor's Theory X and Theory Y Quiz

1. A manager believes employees are lazy and need strict supervision. This aligns with:

Answer: a) Theory X
Explanation: Theory X assumes employees are inherently lazy, lack ambition, and require close supervision and control to ensure productivity. The manager's belief reflects these assumptions.

2. In a tech startup, employees are given flexible hours and creative freedom. This aligns with:

Answer: b) Theory Y
Explanation: Theory Y assumes employees are self-motivated, enjoy their work, and thrive in environments that offer autonomy and responsibility. Flexible hours and creative freedom align with this approach.

3. A factory supervisor uses threats of penalties to ensure workers meet quotas. This reflects:

Answer: a) Theory X
Explanation: Using threats and penalties to enforce productivity is a hallmark of Theory X, which assumes employees need external control and coercion to perform effectively.

4. A manager encourages team brainstorming sessions to solve problems. This is an example of:

Answer: b) Theory Y
Explanation: Theory Y emphasizes employees' creativity and problem-solving abilities. Encouraging brainstorming sessions reflects trust in employees' initiative and collaborative potential.

5. A retail manager assumes employees only work for money and avoid responsibility. This aligns with:

Answer: a) Theory X
Explanation: Theory X assumes employees are motivated primarily by extrinsic rewards like money and avoid responsibility, requiring strict oversight to ensure performance.

6. A project leader delegates tasks and trusts employees to meet deadlines. This reflects:

Answer: b) Theory Y
Explanation: Theory Y assumes employees are responsible and capable of working without close supervision. Delegating tasks and trusting employees aligns with this perspective.
